Why providers send orders to us

A Standard Written Order shouldn't take three rounds of faxes.

Most spinal DME still moves on a fax-back SWO: a partly-filled form goes out, comes back missing a required element, goes out again. Peterson is rebuilding that flow — verified provider identity, orders that are complete at the moment they're signed, and an archive you never have to think about.

Until the provider portal launches, ordering is still simple — fax, email, or call — and we do the chasing, not your office.

    Built around the rules that govern DMEPOS

    The constants we don't drift on

    Spinal DME lives or dies on documentation. These are the federal rules our process is built on — the same ones the coverage assistant cites.

    The SWO's six elements

    Every order carries the beneficiary's name or MBI, the order date, an item description, quantity, the practitioner's name or NPI, and the practitioner's signature.

    CMS Article A55426

    Face-to-face within 6 months

    For applicable items, the treating practitioner's face-to-face encounter must be documented within the six months before the order.

    42 CFR 410.38

    Records kept for a decade

    CMS requires DMEPOS suppliers to retain documentation for at least seven years; Peterson keeps it for ten.

    42 CFR 424.57(c)(9)
