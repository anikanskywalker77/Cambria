The SWO, in plain terms

What a Standard Written Order has to include

Per CMS Article A55426, a valid SWO carries six elements. Miss one and the order isn't complete:

  1. The beneficiary's name or Medicare Beneficiary Identifier (MBI)
  2. The order date
  3. A general description of the item — HCPCS code, brand/model, or a narrative description
  4. Quantity, if applicable
  5. The treating practitioner's name or NPI
  6. The treating practitioner's signature

Separately, applicable items require a face-to-face encounter documented within the six months before the order (42 CFR 410.38), and certain spinal orthoses require a Written Order Prior to Delivery and prior authorization — see the spinal orthoses page.

Coming — the provider portal

A verified web portal, with roster upload

We're building a portal to replace the fax-back loop entirely. Here's the shape of it:

Verified accounts

Prescriber accounts are checked against the NPPES registry and the OIG exclusion list at signup, with multi-factor sign-in and short idle timeouts. Office staff can prepare orders; only the credentialed physician can sign.

Roster upload

Upload a patient roster as a spreadsheet and the portal builds a draft order for each row — pre-checked for the obvious problems (state, face-to-face window, code validity). The physician then signs each order individually, as Medicare requires; the portal just removes the busywork around it.

Guided signing

A signing flow walks the physician through every valid draft, re-authenticating periodically, and produces a signed PDF with a certificate of completion for each order.

Automatic archive

On signature, the order is delivered to our orders queue, written to the records archive at a predictable path, and tracked to fulfillment — with a tamper-evident audit log behind it.

Want your clinic on the early-access list? Tell us about your practice — which product lines you order, roughly what volume, and who'd be the signing physician(s) — and we'll reach out as the pilot opens.
